EASTWAR Dev Log #001

The Reckless Beginning — A Solo Writer Makes a Game

April 22, 2026

It started with KOEI's Romance of the Three Kingdoms III in college. I was hooked from the first play session. Decades later — through RTK14 and the RTK8 Remake — I'm still a die-hard fan.

And for all those years, a quiet desire lived in the back of my mind.

"Someday, I want to make a game like this."

There was a problem. I couldn't draw, couldn't code, couldn't compose or record music. I was just a writer. A solo writer.

Then 2026 happened. AI capabilities leaped forward in a way that made the impossible feel possible. The dream suddenly had a door.

At first, I was afraid. "Can I actually pull this off?"

What gave me the push was something I'd done from late January to March — I built my first ever app from scratch. Grand Slam, a tennis club management app. My first vibe coding project, starting from absolute zero. That process taught me the real limits and possibilities of vibe coding firsthand. A game was a completely different beast: graphics, systems, OST, all of it. But I made a decision I always come back to in moments like this:

"Failing after trying still puts you one step higher than never trying at all."

On April 22, 2026, the reckless challenge began.

Not a Three Kingdoms Clone

After decades of playing these games, one frustration never went away: why only China? Why isn't there a game that spans all of East Asia — the Korean Peninsula and the Japanese archipelago included?

The concept came together quickly.

"China, Korea, Japan — who will be the first to unite all three lands?"

Not just the heroes of the Three Kingdoms era, but the great warriors and strategists who shaped the entire East Asian world — all on the same battlefield. That became the soul of EASTWAR.

First Move — The Poster

Before building the game, the world needed to be visible.

Using AI image generation, I created portraits for each hero. The lead is Yi Sun-sin. His Turtle Ship taking center stage on the poster was never in question — naval warfare is at the heart of the SamgukWAR concept.

Five heroes. One poster.

They met on a single poster. EASTWAR had taken its first step.

Next log: The first week of actual development — designing the battle system from scratch.

Five Heroes · 등장 영웅 5인

Yi Sun-sin

Supreme Commander, Joseon Navy · 이순신

Korea's undefeated naval strategist. Never lost a single battle during the Imjin War. The central hero of EASTWAR — commanding the seas with the Turtle Ship, his dominion over water makes him the most dangerous force on any map.

Guan Yu

Legend of Shu Han · 관우

The God of War. Loyal general of Shu Han, deified across East Asia after his death. His name alone was enough to make armies tremble — and in EASTWAR, that legend returns to the battlefield.

Zhang Fei

Fierce General of Shu Han · 장비

Held back an entire army at Changban Bridge — alone. Fierce and fearless, completely loyal to his sworn brothers. Where strategy ends, Zhang Fei begins.

Oda Nobunaga

Revolutionary of Sengoku Japan · 오다 노부나가

Japan's great revolutionary — the man who burned the old order and came closest to unifying the archipelago. A ruthless visionary who rewrote what was possible. In EASTWAR, he bets everything once more.

Jeong Do-jeon

Architect of Joseon · 정도전

Built a new dynasty through law, philosophy, and institutions. A pen mightier than any sword. In an age of war, his strategies cut deeper than any blade — and his vision extends further than any horizon.
