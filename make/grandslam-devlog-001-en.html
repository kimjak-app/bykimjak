Grand Slam Dev Log #001

First Encounter with Vibe Coding — How a Weather App Started Everything

January 2026

In January 2026, a YouTube video stopped me cold.

"You can build apps with AI without knowing how to code at all."

Vibe Coding. A term I'd never heard before that day. Describe what you want to an AI in plain language, and it writes the code. I'd never studied programming. But building an app without coding?

I had to try.

The First App — Wednesday Weather

What to build came quickly. Something I actually needed. Something I wondered about every week.

For almost three years, I've played tennis every Wednesday morning at the Gwangmyeong Municipal Tennis Court. Living in Seoul and driving out to Gwangmyeong every week — the weather was always on my mind. Rain? Strong winds? I'd shown up to an empty court more times than I could count.

"Let me build an app that shows me the Gwangmyeong weather every Wednesday."

A simple, clear goal. Something I'd actually use myself.

MyWeather — The First Vibe Coding Result

I told ChatGPT what I wanted: a Wednesday weather app for Gwangmyeong.

My requirements were simple:

  • Show me the hours around my tennis time — 8AM to 12PM, with some buffer before and after
  • Display weather conditions, rainfall, and wind speed clearly at a glance
  • Let me check not just this week, but next week too

ChatGPT wrote the code. A single HTML file. I had no idea what any of it meant. I just pasted it in and ran it.

The screen came to life.

Then ChatGPT added something I never asked for.

A dynamic message system — analyzing the weather data and generating a personalized one-liner for the day's conditions. Entirely ChatGPT's idea.

Rain: "Looks like rain or snow. Tennis might be tough today. ☔"
High winds: "It's quite windy! Don't forget your windbreaker! 💨"
Cold: "It's really cold out. Grab a hand warmer and some medicine just in case! ☕"
Clear skies: "Beautiful clear day with no wind! Grab your sunglasses! 😎"

An app built exactly for me. With a bonus touch from ChatGPT that I never requested but immediately loved. Not a single line of code written by hand — and something real was running in front of me.

"So this is vibe coding."

Try MyWeather App →

Toward Grand Slam

After MyWeather was done, I wanted more. It felt too small to stop there.

There's a pattern that repeats every week on the tennis court. Teams get picked, doubles matches get played, and when it's over — nothing is recorded. Who won, who's improving, who's the strongest player in the group. It all just evaporates. That always bothered me.

"Let me add a feature that tracks wins and losses and shows a live ranking."

That would make it something more than a weather app — a place to record results, track standings, and check the forecast all in one. The name had to change too. And the name that came to mind was Grand Slam.

That was Grand Slam's first step.
